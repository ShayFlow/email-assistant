# My Email Style Guide

## General tone
- Professional but human
- Concise
- Natural
- No AI vibe
- No overexplaining

## What I prefer
- Clear replies
- Short paragraphs
- Practical wording
- Friendly but not too warm
- Direct answers

## What I avoid
- Corporate fluff
- Inventing information
- Overly formal language
- Long explanations
- Dramatic wording

## Structure

**Greeting:**
- If the sender's name is in the email: "Hi [Name],"
- If no name is available: "Hi,"

**Body:**
- Answer directly, no preamble
- Take ownership immediately when action is needed
- Anticipate the likely follow-up and address it in the same email
- One blank line between every paragraph

**Closing:**
- Always end with one of these two lines:
  - "Please let me know if you need anything further."
  - "Please let me know if you need anything else."
- Then: "Best regards,"
- Never include my name — Outlook adds the signature automatically

## Common patterns I use
- Brief apology for delay when relevant
- Confirm receipt or status clearly
- Take action language: "I will...", "I'll take care of..."
- Confirmation language: "I can confirm that..."
- Anticipate follow-up: "Please let me know if you're still having trouble after that."
- For visit-related emails: "You are welcome to stop by..."
- Avoid overpromising

## Office hours
Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm. Use this when replying to emails about drop-offs, cheques, or in-person visits.

## Example phrases I like
- I can confirm that
- I will take care of
- I'll resend
- You are welcome to stop by
- At the moment,
- Please let me know if you need anything further.
- Please let me know if you need anything else.
- I will follow up once I have an update.
- I have received
- I have updated
- I apologize for the delay.

## Example phrases I dislike
- I hope this email finds you well
- We are thrilled
- Kindly be advised
- Per my previous email
- Please do not hesitate to contact me
- Happy to help
- Great question
- Absolutely
- It's worth noting that
- While X, it's also important to Y

## Hard formatting rules
- Never use em dashes (—). Restructure the sentence or use a period.
- Never include my name at the bottom.
- One blank line between every paragraph — paste-ready for Outlook.
- Always "Hi [Name]," or "Hi," to open.
